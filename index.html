Need help applying for PIP or ESA?

C-App is a digital advocacy service designed to assist people applying for disability related benefits. Because the Disability Living Allowance is ending for people born after 8 April 1948, there are new benefits you can apply for. These are the Personal Independence Payment (PIP) and Employment and Support Allowance (ESA). The Department for Work and Pensions may have already written to you about your options.

We understand that new benefits can be confusing, and the eligibility and assessment process overwhelming. You can get a friend to go to your assessment with you, but the c-App sites are here to help you too. They give you the information you need to know and show you the types of questions that get asked at assessment.

You can take your time going through the questions, so you can identify the ones that are most important to you before you go.

If you can’t work because of illness or disability, or need help so you can work if you want to, you could be eligible for ESA. You can apply for it if you’re employed, self-employed or unemployed.

This benefit helps with some of the extra costs caused by long-term ill-health or a disability if you’re aged 16 to 64. The rate depends on how your condition affects you, not the condition itself.
